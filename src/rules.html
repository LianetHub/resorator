Service and Safety Rules

The terms used:

Wakesurfing is a water sport in which a rider trails behind a boat, riding the boat's wake without being directly pulled by the boat. After getting up on the wake, typically by use of a tow rope, the wakesurfers will drop the rope, and ride the steep face below the wave's peak in a fashion reminiscent of surfing. Wakesurfers generally use special boards, designed specifically for wakes.

Wakeboarding is a water sport in which the rider, standing on a wakeboard (a board with foot bindings), is towed behind a motorboat across its wake and especially up off the crest in order to perform aerial maneuvers. A hallmark of wakeboarding is the attempted performance of midair tricks. Wakeboarding was developed from a combination of water skiing, snowboarding, and surfing techniques.
A Wakeboard is a short, wide board with fixed boots used in the sport of wakeboarding.

A Wakesurf is a board for the sport of wakesurfing. Surf style - the board is more than 2.5 cm thick with minimum 2 fins at least 5.5 cm high; Skim style - the board is less than 2.5 cm thick with one small or no fin.

The Sports equipment includes any equipment designed or approved for use in the club - wakesurf (skim style or surf style), longboard, hydrofoil, and more, as long as it is not hazardous and has been approved by the Club Administration.

The Boat is a towboat designed for wakeboarding or wakesurfing.

The Club is the place where services are provided.

The Course the water area in which the riding takes place.

The Rider is a customer or a visitor who is wakesurfing.

The Customer is a person who has agreed to join the public offer when booking physical activity and sports services and has paid the cost of a single training session or a deposit.

The Pilot is the person who drives the towboat.

The Coach/ Instructor is the person who professionally assists and coordinates the Rider (announcing the start and the end of the Training, assisting with catching the rope and giving hints on riding technique).

The Tow Rope is a rope with a handle attached to the Boat with which the Rider moves behind the Boat.

A Training /Set /Slot is a minimum booking time.

The Provider is a wakesurf club.

3. The Rules of Behavior when Travelling behind a Towboat:

  1. Regardless of level of training and skills, the Rider must wear (use) personal lifesaving equipment (lifejacket).
  2. Start only on a first-come, first-served basis and as directed by the Instructor or Pilot.
  3. Wakesurfing lessons for Riders under 18 years old are obligatory with an Instructor. If not, only under the supervision of a responsible adult who undertakes all risks.
  4. In case of accidents the witness or participant in the accident must notify the Club staff immediately.
  5. In case of a fall at all stages of the track, the board shall be raised so that the Rider can be seen and wait until the Boat is stern to the Rider.
  6. In case of an emergency, the instructions of the Instructor and the Pilot shall be strictly obeyed.
  7. It is forbidden to wrap the wakesurf rope around the arm or other parts of the body.
  8. After the start and stabilization on the wave, the wakesurf rope shall be thrown over the wave (over the wave crest).
  9. In case of falling off the board, the Rider shall cover his head with his hands.
  10. In case of an unforeseen stop of the Boat, the Rider shall, if possible, change the direction of his movement with the bow of the sport equipment to the left or right of the Boat (off the side of the wave).
